## `<entity-type>` (required) [high priority]

**Purpose:** Categorizes what kind of entity this is.

A short label identifying the category of entity. This helps writers quickly understand what they're dealing with.

**When creating:**
Use a simple, descriptive label. Common types include: location, city, country, region, organization, faction, religion, artifact, landmark, institution, species, language, tradition, or custom types as needed.

**Good examples:**
- location
- city
- organization
- religion
- artifact
- faction
- landmark

## `<summary>` (required) [high priority]

**Purpose:** Brief description for quick reference.

**Format:** 1-2 sentences

A concise summary that captures the entity's essential identity and role in the story world. This is what writers need to understand the entity quickly.

**When creating:**
Write 1-2 sentences that answer: What is this? Why does it matter to the story? Focus on what's distinctive and relevant.

**Good examples:**
- The Grand Bazaar is Constantinople's sprawling covered market, where merchants from across the known world trade everything from silk to secrets.
- The Silk Guild controls textile trade in the city, wielding economic power that rivals the nobility.
- Mount Karthax is an active volcano that the local population believes houses their fire god.
